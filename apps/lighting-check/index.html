What Lighting Check does

Lighting Check measures approximate light level in lux using your device camera. It is designed to help you understand whether a space is bright enough for common settings.

The app includes environment standards for different locations, including cattle farm contexts, so readings can be compared with a target range.

How Lighting Check works

  1. Choose the environment or location you want to compare against.
  2. Point the rear camera at the area you want to measure.
  3. Tap Start and hold the device steady during sampling.
  4. Review the average lux, verdict, samples and chart.
  5. Measure again if the lighting changes or you want another comparison.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Lighting Check?

Lighting Check is an app that estimates light intensity in lux using your device camera.

How do I measure light with Lighting Check?

Point the camera at the area, tap Start and hold the device steady during the measurement.

Does Lighting Check use the rear camera?

Yes. The rear camera is used for best accuracy.

Are the readings as accurate as a professional lux meter?

No. Readings depend on your device camera and should be treated as estimates, not certified measurements.

Can Lighting Check compare readings with standards?

Yes. It includes standards for different locations so you can compare each area against target lux values.

Is Lighting Check free?

Yes. It is free to download with in-app purchases.

Important notes

Lighting Check provides camera-based estimates and is not a replacement for calibrated professional measurement equipment.

Accuracy depends on device hardware, camera exposure, lighting conditions and how steadily the device is held.
